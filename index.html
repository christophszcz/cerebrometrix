Brain Segmentation Simplified

efficient

Cerebrometrix provides fast, efficient, and reliable manual brain segmentations for Magnetic Resonance Images (MRI).

manual segmentation

A key component of many MRI brain studies involve the manual identification & delineation of specific anatomical areas by an experienced anatomist. In this way, a variety of measures can be drawn from segmentations, including volume, morphology, & shape metrics.

cost-effective

Often a laborious and time-intensive process, Cerebrometrix aims to create a cost-effective solution for the manual segmentation required for any given research project.

experts

Our qualified team of anatomical experts can complete manual segmentation projects for a variety of MRI image types, brain structures, and populations, not limited to humans.

What We Do

Cerebrometrix provides you with manual MRI segmentations for any structure of choice within the human brain.

Our trained & experienced team is capable of providing accurate neuroanatomical segmentations using any protocol of your choosing documented in the scientific literature.

If you are in need of segmenting a structure without a clear or existing protocol, our trained team can design & create a viable & appropriate segmentation protocol fit for your specific investigation. Striving for anatomical accuracy, Cerebrometrix employs the use anatomical papers, as well as MRI & histological atlases to help create a viable & reliable segmentation protocol right for you.

Some of the most frequently requested neuroanatomical regions for manual segmentation include, but are not limited to:

Whole-Hippocampal Segmentation

Arguably one of the most popular brain regions, the hippocampus is well-known for its role in memory functions. With an ever-increasing amount of research studies investigating the hippocampus, inquiry with respect to its volumetric & structural changes have identified its involvement in numerous neuropathological diseases & lifestyle factors. For example, changes in whole-hippocampal volume itself have been well-documented in depression (Taylor et al., 2014;Videbech & Ravnkilde, 2004), schizophrenia (Adriano et al., 2012;van Erp et al., 2016), Alzheimer’s Disease (Frisoni, et al., 2008; van de Pol et al., 2006;Wang et al., 2009), & have even been associated with increased physical exercise (Erickson et al., 2011;ten Brinke et al., 2015). Cerebrometrix is capable of delivering whole-hippocampal segmentations for a variety of population types & upholds extensive experience in many frequently used segmentation protocols including: Boccardi et al., 2015, Pruessner et al., 2000,Watson, Jack, & Cendes, 1997, etc.

Hippocampal Subfield Segmentation

With the advent of high resolution MRI, it is now possible to image structures at an unprecedented level of detail. The hippocampus itself is composed of multiple and distinct subfields. Recently, much attention has been placed towards understanding the roles of these subfields within the context of health and disease. Given that whole-hippocampal segmentation features the combination of all subfields together, their individual delineation may provide insight into which specific structures are implicated in observed volume changes. In fact, studies employing manual segmentation of hippocampal subfields have already identified their differential contribution to the volume changes observed in post-traumatic stress disorder (Wang et al., 2010), aging (La Joie et al., 2010), and Alzheimer’s disease (La Joie et al., 2013; Wisse et al., 2014). To date, detailed segmentation of such regions remains dominated by manual segmentation. Our team at Cerebormetrix is experienced in handling high resolution images and can provide subfield segmentation for various manual segmentation protocols including: Palombo et al., 2013, Winterburn et al., 2013, Yuskevich et al., 2015, etc.

Extra-Hippocampal White Matter Segmentation

While the hippocampus has been revered for its importance in memory, disease, and neurological conditions alike, its output pathways remain frequently understudied. Gaining recent popularity, the study of extra-hippocampal white mater structures (i.e. alveus, fimbria, and fornix) occurs most often through diffusion weighted imaging (DWI) studies. Due to difficulties in DWI, many studies restrict investigation to the largest pathway, the fornix, and yet, have demonstrated its decreased integrity in mental disorders such as schizophrenia (Lee et al., 2013), to eating disorders such as anorexia nervosa (Nagahara et al., 2014). Volumetric investigation itself has also implicated extra-hippocampal white matter in Alzheimer’s disease (Copenhaver et al., 2006) and may serve as a predictor of cognitive decline (Fletcher et al., 2013). With its director and founder responsible for the development of a novel extra-hippocampal white matter segmentation protocol (Amaral et al., 2014) Cerebrometrix is capable of delivering extra-hippocampal white matter segmentations using this protocol or others documented in the literature: Amaral et al., 2014; Bilir et al., 1998; Copenhaver et al., 2006;Zahajszky et al., 2001).

Medial Temporal Lobe Cortex Segmentation

The medial temporal lobe cortices include the perirhinal cortex, entorhinal cortex, and the parahippocampal cortex. These cortices have previously been shown to be important in various cognitive processes including object recognition, visiospatial memory functions, and the encoding and translational process of memory formation (Squire et al., 2004; Wolk et al., 2011). Volumetric and structural studies have also identified these cortices as key players in memory and various forms of dementia (Dickerson et al., 2009; Frisoni et al., 1999; Krumm et al., 2016). Cerebrometrix is capable of providing medial temporal lobe cortex segmentations using established segmentation protocols for both standard (e.g. Pruessner et al., 2002), as well as high resolution MRI images (e.g. Amaral et al., 2015, Palombo et al., 2013).

Basal Ganglia and Striatum Segmentation

The basal ganglia and striatum are a collection of related subcortical nuclei important for motor movements, cognition, emotion, motivation, and reward. Structural investigation of these regions has also indicated their involvement in Parkinson’s disease (Geng, Li, & Zee, 2006) and Autism Spectrum Disorder (Sussman et al., 2015). Cerebrometrix can provide segmentations for all structures comprising the basal ganglia and striatum including: the globus pallidus, nucleus accumbens, ventral pallidum, caudate nucleus, putamen, substantia nigra, and subthalamic nucleus.

Ventricular Segmentation

The brain contains four interconnected ventricles that are filled with cerebrospinal fluid. Not only is ventricular size involved in diseases of the ventricular system such as meningitis and ventriculitis (Sporrborn et al., 2015), ventricular enlargement has also been observed in mental disorders including schizophrenia (Wright et al., 2000) and dementia (Nestor et al., 2008). The segmentation of these ventricles may also serve as a predictor of disease risk or onset. Cerebrometrix can provide you with ventricular segmentations for cases of mild ventricular enlargement, or for more extreme cases such as those observed in hydrocephalus.

Whole Brain Segmentation

Whole brain segmentation involves the isolation of non-brain tissues (e.g. the skull) such that only brain tissue can be measured. Brain extraction is a key step in the analysis of MRI images and can be used to provide measures of total brain volume. Although automatic methods exist for whole brain segmentation (Battaglini et al., 2008; Xue et al., 2007), manual segmentation remains a gold standard and is a requirement in special or patient cases. Cerebrometrix can not only provide whole brain segmentations, but can also quality control and manually correct whole brain segmentation outputs from automatic pipelines.

Lesions and Brain Tumor Segmentation

In special patient cases, manual segmentation of brain lesions is required for classification, diagnosis, and/or prognosis. In addition, delineation and characterization of lesions may also serve as an aid during surgery or for preoperative surgical planning. Cerebrometrix can provide you with segmentations for a variety of brain lesions including white matter lesions and brain tumors.
